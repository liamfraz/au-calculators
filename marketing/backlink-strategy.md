### Reddit Communities

Target subreddits (see `/marketing/reddit-posts.md` for full drafts):

- **r/AusFinance** (~500k members) — The primary AU personal finance subreddit. High engagement on property, HECS, super topics
- **r/AusProperty** (~60k members) — Property-focused. Stamp duty and mortgage questions daily
- **r/fiaustralia** (~100k members) — FIRE community. HECS payoff strategy and super projection discussions

**Rules:**
- Account must have established history before posting tool links
- Provide genuine value in the post — the calculator is supplementary, not the point
- Never post the same link in multiple subreddits on the same day
- Engage in comments on your posts — don't drop and run

### Whirlpool.net.au Forum Threads

Whirlpool is Australia's largest tech/finance forum. Target existing threads where people are asking questions our calculators answer.

**Draft Reply 1 — Stamp Duty Thread**
> Search for threads titled "stamp duty calculator" or "stamp duty [state]"

Reply:
> Hey, I've been looking into this too. The tricky part is each state has completely different thresholds and concessions — for example, a $650k property in NSW vs VIC can differ by over $10k in stamp duty depending on whether you qualify as a first home buyer.
>
> I found this breakdown helpful for comparing across states: https://au-calculators.vercel.app/calculators/stamp-duty — it covers all states including the FHB exemptions and concessions.
>
> For your situation specifically, it's worth checking whether you qualify for the FHB concession in your state, since the thresholds changed recently.

**Draft Reply 2 — HECS/HELP Repayment Thread**
> Search for threads about "HECS repayment" or "HELP debt"

Reply:
> The 2025-26 repayment thresholds changed again — the minimum repayment threshold is now around $54k. What catches people off guard is how quickly the rate jumps: going from $54k to $60k salary can mean an extra $600+/year in repayments.
>
> If you want to model what happens at different salary levels, this calculator shows the repayment brackets clearly: https://au-calculators.vercel.app/calculators/hecs-help
>
> Whether voluntary repayments make sense depends on your interest rate on other debt. If you've got a HECS balance under the indexation rate and no other high-interest debt, it's usually not worth paying off early since indexation is pegged to CPI.

**Draft Reply 3 — Mortgage Comparison Thread**
> Search for threads about "mortgage calculator" or "how much can I borrow"

Reply:
> Something a lot of first-time buyers miss is how much the total interest cost changes with even small rate differences. The difference between 5.5% and 6.0% on a $500k loan over 30 years is about $55k in total interest — that's a car.
>
> I've been using this to run different scenarios: https://au-calculators.vercel.app/calculators/mortgage-repayment — you can compare monthly repayments and total cost across different rates and terms.
>
> Also worth checking if your lender offers an offset account — even $20k sitting in offset on a $500k loan saves ~$1,100/year in interest at 6%.

### OzBargain

OzBargain isn't a traditional backlink source, but they have a **Forums** section where financial tools can be shared if positioned as free resources.

**Approach:**
- Post in **Forums > Finance** (not Deals — calculators aren't deals)
- Title: "Free Australian stamp duty calculator with all state rates & FHB concessions"
- Frame as: "Built this free tool, no ads, no data collection — just does the maths"
- OzBargain users are savvy — they'll call out anything that feels like marketing
- Only post once. Engage genuinely in comments
